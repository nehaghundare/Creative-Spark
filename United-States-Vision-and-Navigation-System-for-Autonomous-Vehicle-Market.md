Vision and navigation systems in L3 vehicles integrate high-definition maps, LIDAR, radar, and advanced machine learning algorithms to allow the vehicle to make decisions in real-time. These systems are designed to handle a broader range of driving scenarios without human intervention, although the driver must be able to take control if the system requests. The market for L4 vehicle systems is still in its infancy but is expected to grow significantly as technological advancements and regulatory approval continue to progress. Vision and navigation systems are at the forefront of L4 development, with companies focusing on making these systems more reliable and capable of handling diverse driving conditions. L5 vehicles are capable of complete autonomy, meaning there is no need for a steering wheel, pedals, or any driver input.
